Fruit

In many Latin American regions, fresh fruit is used not only for aesthetics but also to cut through the richness of the cake, creating a perfect balance of flavors. The tradition of pairing cakes with fruit dates back to ancient times, and in tres leches, it adds a refreshing touch to an already indulgent dessert.

Whipped Cream

Whipped cream became a popular cake topping in the early 20th century, prized for its lightness and ability to complement heavier textures. In tres leches, the whipped cream balances the sweetness of the milks and adds a cool, smooth finish.

Sponge Cake

The airy sponge cake absorbs the trio of milks, staying moist and light. Its roots trace back to Latin America, inspired by European soaked cakes. Its structure is key to holding the milk while staying moist yet firm.
